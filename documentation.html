Museum

The organization of items to tell the History of Phallic Culture was thought of as a museum exhibition. One of the inspirations was the Gabinetto Segreto of the National Archaeological Museum of Naples. This section of the MAN contains, since the time of the Bourbon sovereigns of Naples, all works considered erotic or obscene, including some of the testimonies of phallic culture that were selected in this project.

Layout

Trying to reproduce the feeling of starting a visit to a museum, we sought a different layout on this page. By placing the museum plan occupying the entire page, the desire was to prevent the user from having any suggestion of what their visit will be like, seeking to reproduce the feeling of the visitor who finds himself at the entrance of a museum. Even though the navbar is available for the user to access objects in another way, the arrangement of the page's main content occupying the entire screen is an invitation for the user to explore a space freely.

As in many museums, works are presented im rooms according to their type of production: architecture, literary works, paintings, sculptures and frescoes. To achieve this effect, the Museum page was diveded in different columns and rows to replicate the structure of a museum plan. Then, photos of objects of each type were inserted into carousels, allowing the user to have a preview of the objects in each room.
